 2008 Activities

 2008.1.18

Symposium on DASH Diet Design

While the JNC 7 Report has recognized DASH Diet to be critical in hypertension management, a practical DASH diet based on the local eating style should be designed urgently. With intensive participation in diet planning and designing, the symposium has successfully fulfilled DASH diet into a practical dietary pattern with local Taiwanese foods. The diet planning and designing have been highly appreciated by both the nutrition professionals and the general population.     

 2008.3.16

Symposium on Maternal Weight Control and Recovery after Delivery

Traditionally, after delivering a baby, Taiwanese women consume a considerable quantity of high energy foods, such as chicken soup cooked with sesame oil. Therefore, noticeable maternal weight gain has been observed, and become a serious health issue. The symposium aimed to provide a better recovery management and weight control scheme for the women after delivery.

2008.5.30-31

The 34th Annual Meeting of the Nutrition Society of Taiwan

The 34th Annual Meeting of the Nutrition Society of Taiwan was held at I-Shou University, Kaohsiung County. There was a total of 450 member attendance and 200 posters were presented in the Basic and Clinical Nutrition Sectors. 

2008.7.5

Symposium on Weight Management Using Evidence-Based Nutritional Sciences

Many dietary legends are propagandized through news media without solid evidence-based information. The symposium aimed to provide the healthier ways in weight management based on evidence-based data, so that the general population can benefit from such updated without being misled through unhealthy measurements. 

2008.10.2-4

The 1st Taiwan Nutrition Leadership Training Program Introduced by International Union of Nutritional Sciences, IUNS, 21 outstanding candidates were selected and trained for nutritional leadership by 15 speakers from academic and nutrition-related professionals. The delivered topics included: “Interdisciplinary and Integrative Nutrition”, “International Nutrition in Taiwan”, “Nutrition Policy in Taiwan”, “The New Food Crisis”, “Nutritional Crisis and Policy”, “Linking Nutrition and Agriculture”, “Food Safety in Taiwan”, “Biomedical and Health Research in Taiwan”, “Leadership in Nutrition Research”, “Preventive Nutrition”, “Health and Disorders Related to Nutrition”, “Future Perspectives in the Training and Education of Nutrition Leadership”. 

2008. 11.14-17 

2008 International Conference and Exhibition on “Nutraceuticals, Functional Foods

and Dietary Supplements”

The “1st International Conference and Exhibition on Nutraceuticals and Functional Foods” in 2008 was held with great success in Taichung. There were 20 plenary presentations and more than 100 presentations in different professional sessions. About 480 participants from all over the world joined and enjoyed this conference. 

2008.12.9

Symposium on Nutrition Policy in Taiwan

Nutrition professionals gathered to discuss extensively on the nutrition policy in Taiwan. Issues included “Optimal Nutrition for the Health and Disease”, “Nutrition Education in Schools”, “Food Safety”, and “Nutrition Research and Preventive Medicine”.  

2009 Activities           

2009. 3.8,15,29

Training Program of Nutrition Therapy on Acute Renal Failure and Kidney

Transplantation

Immediate and intensive nutritional therapy is required for patients suffering from acute renal failure and for those undertaken kidney transplantation. The training program aimed to equip the nutrition professionals with the most advanced diet therapy knowledge and practical skills.

 2009.5.22-23

The 35th Annual Meeting of the Nutrition Society of Taiwan

The 2009 Annual Meeting was held at Providence University, Taichung City. Two series of presentation were included: “DHA and its applications”, and “Dietary Guidelines and Reference Book in Taiwan”. Around 500 members attended and 250 posters were displayed in basic and clinical nutrition divisions. Student member competitions by oral or poster presentations were held, and the winners received awards.

The members of the 15th Councils were elected successfully.

 2009.6.

Symposium on National Nutrition Policy, Dietary Guidelines and Reference Book in Taiwan

The Dietary Guidelines in Taiwan are in a process of revision. The current “Guidelines” were published in 1995. Since then, the dietary and living conditions of Taiwan people have changed dramatically and the disease patterns altered accordingly. The symposium was to fulfill the gap between the old and the current situations, and to provide more optimal guidelines according to current status. The newly-revised version will put more emphasis on physical activity and the prevention of chronic diseases.

 2009.7.18

Symposium on DASH Diet and Hypertension Control Diet

The results of Nutrition and Health Survey in Taiwan (NAHSIT, 2005-2008) showed that the incidence of hypertension in the age group between 19 and 50 was 17.5%, and 38.5% in 50 or above. The symposium was held to promote DASH diet for hypertension prevention and treatment.

 Ongoing Events

Drafting “National Nutrition Policy”

NUTRITION SOCIETY OF TAIWAN (NST) set up a task force in 2008 to conduct a series of activities in following years to prepare a draft of “National Nutrition Policy”. The activities include surveys on the current nutrition status in Taiwan, and comparing the survey results to those of the developed countries, and offering advice to relevant agencies, departments and organizations. They meet on a regular basis to work together. Now the 2nd version of the draft is under review. It consists of 8 chapters and 23 sections. After a circulation in relevant government sectors, hopefully it will serve as a major draft for legislation.
